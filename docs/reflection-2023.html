Draft 1

So, it’s been a year.6 I’m planning to work on this in a few drafts, so let’s take a page from last year’s yearly reflection and do five things I was grateful for last year and five things I’m excited for this year.

Last year I thought that I would be excited for:

  • Passing my third year exam. I did it! I’m very proud of myself, and it’s wild that I’m now a dissertator

  • Self publishing a book. As of the time that I’m drafting this right now, I’m sitting at just under three hundred and fifty thousand words, I have 1351 followers, and my chapters have been read an average of over four thousand times each.7 It’s been a great experience, and I have definitely been forced8 to reflect on myself based on how the writing I put out is received.

  • Taking an improv class. Sadly, this never happened. I do think that I went to an improv show last year, which was fun. Hopefully I’ll find a time with my friend to go to improv class this year.

  • Watching my littlest brother graduate. He did! It was fantastic to get to watch him, and it was really great getting to spend time with family and see him in his element.

  • Growing in my relationships. Oh boy did I! I started being deliberate about writing letters to friends, and I made an effort to stay in touch with a lot of people. I think that they have by and large been successful goals, though I’ve also finally come to the realization that I don’t have to be friends with everyone. Some people will dislike me for who I am, and that’s ok.9

All of those tasks that I did accomplish were things that I am glad I did. Other highlights from the year,10

  • I got to explore my new home state and visit a lot of the state parks in it as outreach!

  • I got to give a lecture to a classroom11

  • I did open mics!

  • I kind of became a computational chemist

  • I got much better at writing! Both in terms of content and in terms of quality.12

That is all very exciting! What am I looking forward to in 2024?

  • Publishing a first author paper!13

  • Growing in my relationships.14

  • Giving an invited talk at a university15

  • I’m excited to watch my littlest brother graduate again!16

  • I’m excited to write and put out an album.17

Ok cool, that also includes some of my goals for 2024. Turns out I did set goals for 2023, I just put them in a separate document. My goals last year were to:

  • Blog Daily. I did better at that towards the end of the year, but I think that I averaged somewhere like one day in three overall.18

  • Do BiaY and CCCiaY. I completely dropped the ball on both of these. I do think that they’re important, and a family member is doing them, so that should help be a motivator.

  • I wanted to be better about exercise. I think that I ebbed and flowed in that goal over the course of the year. I’m generally pretty ok with that fact, though. I have higher priorities, and I’m comfortable with that fact.

  • I wanted to write more and varied forms of poetry and prose. I think that I generally did a pretty good job of writing more, for all that I kind of dropped the ball on the poetry. I think that I did do a pretty good job with varied forms of prose.19

  • I wanted to practice music more. Again, this is a goal that ebbed and flowed, but I would like it to flow more than ebb this coming year.

  • I wanted to try monthly, quarterly, and yearly goals. I generally did alright20 with the monthly goals, but I gave up on quarterly goals almost instantly and never really referenced the yearly goals.

Last year I wanted to make an effort to learn species counterpoint better. I don’t think that this is a goal that I am going to take with me to 2024. I’ve briefly talked about it before, but to recap: I do want to compose, but mostly for my own solo performance, rather than traditional choral composition. I don’t think that there are enough hours in the day, days in the week, or weeks in the year for me to explore everything that I want, so I need to prioritize.

What else are things that I want to work on or do this year?

I said in my 25 for 25 post that I want to learn how to weave, swim a mile, take an improv class, learn a polka on the accordion, and spin yarn. Those all remain things that I think that I want to do. Let’s see what I think that I would need to accomplish each of the tasks:

  • Learn to weave: sign up for a class with my friend who also wants to learn how to weave

  • Swim a mile: Start swimming regularly, focusing on just swimming

  • Take an improv class: force my schedule to overlap with the friend I want to take the course with and the course itself

  • Learn a polka on the accordion: get my accordion back and look up an easy polka for it

  • Spin Yarn: either use the kit that I have or sign up to take a class with a friend

What else do I want to accomplish?

  • I would like to figure out what I want to do after graduate school

  • I would like to finish at least most of my thesis research

  • I would like to make significant progress on writing my thesis

  • I don’t think that I want to do NaNoWriMo again. It was fun the past few years, but I know myself now well enough to know that the way I should grow as a writer is by writing better, not necessarily writing more.

  • On that note, I would like to finish the book on craft and read at least the other books that my friends have given me for Christmas.21

  • I would like to read through a lot of the books that I’ve checked out, and I would like to be able to return them with the knowledge in my mind

  • I would like to be better at putting myself out there, at least in terms of presenting a genuine version of myself more readily.

  • I would like to apply for a job in whatever field I end up deciding feels right for my next steps

Wow that’s a lot of things that I want to do. I guess that I should either reduce the goals or increase my productivity. Those both sound like a lot of work, so we’ll see what ends up happening. For now, time to look away from this and do work for the day, rather than looking months into the future.
